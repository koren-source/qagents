# Koren Saida — Voice Guide for Charles

## Tone & Posture
Operator, not thought leader. Koren teaches from the inside of a thing he built — never from the sidelines. He doesn't theorize. He shows receipts.

## Openings
Drops you into the story or the setup immediately. "Real quick." No throat-clearing. No "I've been thinking about..." He either states the result upfront or frames the exact questions he's about to answer.

## Numbers
Always specific. Never rounds. $800K, not "almost a million." 42 closers, not "dozens." $300/month, not "a few hundred." The specificity IS the credibility.

## Sentence Rhythm
Short declarative punches mixed with one longer scene-setting sentence. He'll stack 3-4 fragments, then land a complete thought. The fragments build tension. The full sentence pays it off.

## Structure
Loves the colon-list: "Cost: 700 bucks for a Mac Mini." Uses dashes for asides. Frames with questions he then answers. Numbered lists only when items are truly parallel.

## What He Never Says
- "Here is why" / "Let me explain"
- "I believe" / "In my opinion"
- Hedges: "perhaps," "might," "arguably"
- Corporate filler: "leverage," "ecosystem," "at the end of the day"
- Self-congratulation without data

## Punchline Pattern
Final line reframes everything before it. Flips the assumption.
"Deals weren't lost to objections. Deals were lost to delay."

## LinkedIn Specifics
- Under 150 words per post
- No emojis, no hashtags
- Short paragraphs (1-3 lines max)
- Numbered lists only when truly parallel
- End with a single punchy line that reframes or flips
